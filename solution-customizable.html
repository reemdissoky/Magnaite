When the standard stack doesn't fit

Most operations look standard. Yours probably isn't.

Off-the-shelf systems integration assumes the operation it's deployed in resembles every other operation. In Saudi enterprise, that assumption breaks more often than it holds. The mall has a tenant configuration nobody planned for. The QSR network runs three legacy POS systems alongside the new one. The government deployment requires a hardware spec that didn't exist when the contract was signed.

The integrators who can't adapt walk away from these conversations. The ones who can — that's the work we've been doing for thirty-five years.

What we build

Custom integrations, sector-specific architectures, bespoke deployments.

Custom Magnaite engagements often look like one of these: a multi-vendor integration where existing equipment has to talk to new infrastructure; a sector-specific configuration where the standard product needs adaptation; a bespoke architecture where the requirement is unique enough to need a designed-for-you build.

Whatever the shape — the engagement starts the same way. A solutions architect on the phone, an honest read of what's actually needed, and a written assessment within days. If we're not the right fit for the custom build, we'll tell you. If we are, the build starts.

One firm, one accountability line

Custom builds notoriously involve multiple vendors and unclear ownership. With Magnaite, you have one team responsible for the whole architecture — from design through long-term support.

We don't ask you to rip and replace what's working. The custom build adapts to your existing equipment, workflows, and partnerships — extending what works rather than discarding it.

Sized to your phase

Custom doesn't have to mean expensive. We size the engagement to the actual scope — proof of concept, pilot deployment, or full custom build. Your phase determines the spend.

Long-term support contract

Custom builds need custom maintenance. The team that designed it is the team that supports it. Standard SLA, kingdom-wide.
