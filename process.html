How we work

Eight steps.
Same eight.
Every client.

We don't sell off a price list. Every Magnaite engagement starts the same way: with a conversation about what you're actually trying to make work, then a careful read of where you are right now. The deployment that follows is sized to your phase — not a template lifted from someone else's project.

The journey

From first conversation
to year-ten support call.

The first four steps cost you nothing. The next four are where Magnaite earns the partnership. Every step is a chance for either side to walk away — that's why the ones who stay, stay for a decade.

01
Free

Initial meeting

A thirty-minute conversation — phone, video, or in person at your site or ours. We listen first. We understand what you're trying to fix, build, or upgrade. No deck, no pitch, no obligation. If we're not the right fit, we'll say so on this call.

02
Free

Site visit

If the conversation suggests a real opportunity, a Magnaite engineer comes to your location to see the operation firsthand. What's running today? What's missing? What's quietly costing you money? Site visits are unbillable, anywhere in the kingdom.

03
Free

Consultation & assessment

We come back with a written read of your current state, the operational risks we've identified, and the cost-benefit picture for the most relevant solutions. This document is yours to keep — even if you choose not to work with us.

04
Free

Live demo

We show you the systems running — at our office, at one of your branches, or at a current client site. You see the dashboard, the workflow, the actual experience your team would have. Not a sales video. The real product, working.

05
Engagement begins

Solution scoping

If we both agree there's a fit, we sit down to scope the engagement. What do you need today? What do you need in six months? What can we phase in to keep cost in proportion to value? The proposal is sized to your operation — cost-effective for early-stage businesses, comprehensive for kingdom-scale ones, tailored either way.

06
Deployment

Pilot install

For multi-branch operators, we always start with a pilot — typically one or two locations. The team learns. The system gets tuned to your specific operation. The metrics that matter to you start showing up in the dashboard. Issues get caught before they become network-wide problems.

07
Scale

Network rollout

With the pilot validated, the rollout scales — phased clusters, parallel deployments, never a closure. The pilot becomes the standard configuration. New branches deploy faster because there's a model to copy. Existing branches upgrade faster because there's a target to converge to.

08
Ongoing

Monitor & evolve

This is the step most integrators treat as an afterthought. For Magnaite, it's the entire point. 24/7 support. Same-day SLA. Quarterly performance reviews. Ongoing assessment of what's working, what isn't, and what new technologies are worth adopting as the operation evolves. The relationship stays current the same way our team does — by renewing continuously.

The thinking

We assign the right solution
for your phase.

Every business is at a different point in its journey. A coffee chain at three locations needs something different from a QSR network at three hundred. A government building needs something different from a retail floor. A startup needs something different from a thirty-year incumbent.

The first four steps exist so we understand which kind of client you actually are — not so we can sell you the same thing we sold the last person. Some clients want cost-effectiveness above all. Some want AI-first capability. Some want compliance-ready infrastructure. Some want maximum operational visibility. We assign the engagement around what you actually need, and we phase the deployment around what you can actually absorb.

That's why the same eight steps work for a five-branch operator and a hundred-and-forty-branch operator. The steps don't change. The proportion does.

The questions we ask

  • What's the actual operational pain — not the symptom?
  • What phase is your business in right now?
  • What's your real budget, and what's it sized for?
  • What's the compliance environment you operate inside?
  • What does success look like in six months? In three years?
  • Who on your team will live with this system every day?
